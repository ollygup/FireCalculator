Methodology. All figures are in today's MYR (real terms) — return assumptions are net-of-inflation, so the model never needs to inflate expenses forward, and the MYR/USD rate is applied once at today's rate rather than projected. Three phases per plan: Accumulation (current age → working-to age: EPF and Investments both receive contributions), Bridge (working-to age → 55: EPF is locked and earns dividends only, Investments alone fund net expenses — Coast FIRE plans stop contributions from the current age but otherwise follow this same structure), Decumulation (55 → 90: both pots unlocked, withdrawals split proportionally by balance). Barista income, reduced by your fluctuation % under the Conservative scenario, offsets expenses until the Barista end age. The FIRE target is not a single fixed number — it recalculates at every age from your expense and income timelines, so downsizing, other income starting/stopping, or lifestyle changes in later retirement all shift it automatically.
